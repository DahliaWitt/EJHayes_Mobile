Middle School Guide

Welcome to Middle School! E.J. Hayes is a safe and nurturing learning enviroment. Middle school may seem scary, but it's not! The bell rings at 9:05, where you will watch the morning announcements before begining your day. There is seven periods in a day, and you transition to each of them after the bell rings. WEB-Where Everybody Belongs

What is WEB?
  • WEB stands for “Where Everybody Belongs.” At Edythe J. Hayes Middle School WEB means that we welcome and embrace students from diverse backgrounds and who have unique talents, interests and characteristics.
  • WEB is a yearlong program wherein 8th Grade Leaders conduct experiential activities to prepare incoming 6th Graders for middle school. Activities include: A high-energy, inspirational Day 1 Assembly, monthly group activities, peer mentoring/tutoring, and structured gatherings.
The Main Components of WEB
  • 6th Grade Transition: WEB helps incoming 6th Grade Students make a successful transition to middle school through a variety of high-energy, engaging activities and practical tips for success.
  • 8th Grade Leadership Development: WEB promotes Leadership and Service in 8th Grade students by providing training in leadership development, and organizing planned, intentional opportunities to serve incoming 6th Grade students.
  • Creating a Welcoming School Environment: WEB helps to establish a positive climate of cooperation and mutual respect in our school by teaching 8th Graders to treat 6th Graders with respect, and by celebrating the unique talents, interests and characteristics of others.
How it Works
  • WEB Leadership Academy: Selected eighth grade students will have two days of training during the summer to become WEB Leaders (“mentors” to incoming 6th Graders), and will have training/support throughout the year to perform WEB Leader responsibilities.
  • WEB Assembly: On the first day of school all incoming sixth graders will participate in the WEB Assembly (Orientation), and then small group “friend-building” activities.
  • Classroom Follow-Up: WEB Leaders lead monthly Character Ed. lessons for all sixth Grade Classrooms (such as Integrity, Dealing with Rumors/Gossip, Responsibility, etc.).
  • Social Follow-Up: Periodic activities to foster school-connectedness, friendships, etc.
  • WEB 1 to 1: Pairings of WEB Leaders with specific sixth graders who may be struggling with grades, organization, peer relationships, etc.; may happen daily, bi-weekly, etc.
  • Peer Tutoring: WEB Leaders will assist teachers in our new “Academics in Motion” (AIM) tutoring program, a before-school program to provide academic assistance to students in need.

Edythe J. Hayes Middle School, located southeast of town, was established in August 2004. Ours is one of the most technologically advanced middle schools in Kentucky. We have roughly one computer for every two students, including two full computer labs. Every classroom has at least five computer workstations and an LCD projector. Integration of technology is a daily practice and a natural part of instruction, and we continue to push the envelope.

 

What makes our school special

 

Our mission statement charges the faculty and staff with engaging "all students in a safe and nurturing environment that promotes academic excellence and develops responsible, respectful leaders." This statement goes beyond school climate - beyond high academic standards - and combines the two to create a developmentally responsive, socially equitable and academically challenging environment for our students. Our faculty and staff members work together to give every child who passes through our doors the keys to success - not only in school, but also in the community. We believe in building relationships with our students that extend beyond the classroom and promote positive interactions by using our guidelines to success - the Panther Promise: Learn Daily, Laugh Often, Lead Respectfully and Live Responsibly.

Academics

Accelerated Learning Program

In 2010, Edythe J. Hayes Middle School will launch the Accelerated Learning Program.  The Accelerated Learning Program allows for students to be flexibly scheduled into classes depending on need and ability level.

Students are scheduled into one of three areas- enhanced, core, or accelerated- based on their Measure of Academic Progress score, course common assessment scores, and teacher recommendation.  Unlike many programs that track students throughout their courses, students may be accelerated in one subject, but below grade level in another.  They are scheduled accordingly.

Also, students may be moved throughout the year depending on their response to the program.  In addition, we offer a range of intervention classes and programs for students in reading, writing, math, and behavior, both remedial and enrichment in nature.

School-wide Instructional Strategies

We employ several school wide and departmental instructional strategies, in an effort to prepare our studies for rigorous high school instruction.  The basis of these strategies is embedded in a Pre-Advanced Placement curriculum.  The primary goal of these strategies is to increase the number of students that enroll in Advanced Placement courses in high school.

School-Wide Strategies
  • Frayer model vocabulary strategy

This is a vocabulary development tool. In contrast with a straight definition, the model helps to develop a better understanding of complex concepts by having students identify not just what something is, but what something is not.

  • Panther Binder system

Each student is to have a three-inch, three-ring binder in order to teach organizational skills.  The school sells students tabbed dividers with GROW chart for each subject area.  Teachers are expected to develop a table of contents for their portion of the binder, and the table of contents is to be posted in every teacher’s room for students to view.  The binder is checked for a class grade once every mid-term.

  • Cornell Notes

The Cornell method provides a systematic format for condensing and organizing notes.

Exploratory Course Offerings

Exploratory curriculum can be the key to middle school students identifying their own interests beyond the classroom.  They may also appreciate fine arts and applied arts, while developing concepts and skills in the areas selected.  Our students get two exploratory courses per day.  Some are nine week courses, while some may be taken for a full school year.  We offer a variety of courses from the arts and humanities as well as the practicial living and career studies content areas.  During these blocks of time, students may also take advantage of support skills courses, designed to help students develop successful study habits, as well as Response to Intervention courses, which target students with significant skills gaps in reading and math.
